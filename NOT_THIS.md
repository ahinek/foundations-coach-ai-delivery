# Not This

This outline describes stewardship responsibilities in an AI-accelerated delivery environment.

It is equally important to clarify what this is not.

---

## Not a new framework

This is not a replacement for Agile frameworks, the Agile Manifesto, or existing delivery practices.

It does not introduce ceremonies, certifications, or proprietary models.

It is an extension of stewardship, not a reinvention of delivery.

---

## Not a role replacement

This is not intended to replace Scrum Masters, Agile Coaches, Engineering Managers, or Product Owners.

In most environments, these responsibilities will be incorporated into existing roles.

The focus is evolution, not duplication.

---

## Not AI governance or compliance enforcement

This is not a compliance office.

It is not a tool approval committee.

It does not exist to police experimentation or restrict innovation.

Guardrails are about coherence, not control.

---

## Not a productivity accelerator

This role does not exist to extract more output.

It exists to ensure that acceleration rests on shared foundations.

Speed without structure creates hidden costs, e.g. "Building the wrong things faster."

---

## Not centralized control

Stewardship is not micromanagement.

This role does not dictate technical implementation details or override team autonomy.

It surfaces systemic risks, encourages alignment, and protects collaborative health.

---

## Not anti-AI

This outline assumes AI is here to stay.

It does not resist experimentation or technological advancement.

It seeks to ensure that AI strengthens delivery systems rather than fragmenting them.

---

If this outline becomes rigid, bureaucratic, or detached from team reality, it has drifted from its purpose.

AI magnifies what’s beneath it.

So does structure.
