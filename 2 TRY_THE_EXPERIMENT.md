# Try the Experiment

>   
> Strengthen Agile foundations with one team before scaling AI adoption across an organization.

Most organizations are approaching AI adoption as a **tool rollout**.

This repository proposes something different.

AI changes the **speed and scale of delivery**, which means it also amplifies weaknesses in the delivery system: unclear problem framing, fragile ownership, weak feedback loops, and fragmented decision-making.

Rather than attempting to fix these issues after scaling AI, this experiment proposes a small, focused test:

**Embed a Foundations Coach for AI Delivery with one cross-functional team for 8–10 weeks and observe what changes.**

The goal is not to prove a framework.

The goal is to **learn what conditions allow AI to become a force multiplier rather than a source of chaos.**

---

# Who This Is For

This experiment is designed for people who believe that **AI adoption is as much a systems challenge as a technology challenge.**

## Leaders

Engineering and product leaders who want to:

- Increase development speed **without increasing operational risk**
- Introduce AI responsibly
- Reduce rework, thrash, and surprise failures
- Understand what organizational conditions AI actually requires

---

## Delivery Teams

Cross-functional teams who:

- Are beginning to use AI tools in development
- Want to avoid chaotic acceleration
- Want to experiment safely
- Want clarity around how AI fits into team ownership and workflow

---

## Agile Coaches / Technical Leaders

People interested in exploring a new role:

**Foundations Coach for AI Delivery**

A coach embedded with teams to ensure the delivery system remains healthy as AI increases the pace of change.

---

# Signals That AI Is Amplifying Weak Foundations

If your organization is seeing any of the following, AI may already be **amplifying system weaknesses**.

## Problem Framing Issues

- Work begins with **solutions instead of outcomes**
- Acceptance criteria are vague or missing
- AI-generated work must be heavily reworked

---

## Flow Fragmentation

- Teams start more work because AI lowers the cost of starting
- Work-in-progress increases
- Finishing and validating work becomes harder

---

## Decision Bottlenecks

- A few individuals become AI experts and bottlenecks
- Teams depend on heroes instead of shared understanding
- Decision ownership becomes unclear

---

## Feedback Instability

- Code reviews struggle to keep up with AI-generated output
- Test coverage lags behind feature speed
- Bugs appear in clusters after releases

---

## Cognitive Overload

- Developers feel faster but **less confident**
- Product and engineering lose shared understanding
- Teams experience increasing thrash

---

If several of these signals appear, the issue may not be AI itself.

It may be that **AI is exposing weaknesses already present in the delivery system.**

# How to Run the Experiment

This is intentionally **small and lightweight**.

## Step 1 — Select One Team

Choose a **single cross-functional delivery team**:

- Product
- Engineering
- QE / Testing
- UX if applicable

Avoid starting with multiple teams.

The goal is **learning**, not rollout.

---

## Step 2 — Define Success Criteria

Before starting, define a few measurable signals.

### Delivery Signals

- Reduced rework
- More consistent completion of sprint commitments
- Stable cycle time

### Quality Signals

- Fewer escaped defects
- Faster bug resolution
- Stable test coverage

### Team Health Signals

- Increased shared understanding of work
- AI use spread across the team rather than concentrated in one expert
- Lower cognitive stress

Success criteria should include **clear thresholds** for:

- Success  
- Partial success  
- Failure  

The goal is not to prove success — the goal is to **observe honestly**.

---

## Step 3 — Run an 8–10 Week Engagement

Embed a **Foundations Coach for AI Delivery** with the team.

The coach:

- Observes ceremonies and workflow
- Helps the team strengthen Agile fundamentals
- Helps the team introduce AI intentionally
- Surfaces systemic issues safely

The coach does **not** own delivery or enforce compliance.

They strengthen the system that delivery depends on.

---

## Step 4 — Capture What Happened

At the end of the experiment, document:

- What improved
- What got worse
- What surprised the team
- What conditions allowed AI to help or harm

Capture both **quantitative signals and qualitative observations**.

---

## Step 5 — Decide What Happens Next

Possible outcomes include:

- Expanding the experiment to additional teams
- Adjusting leadership behaviors
- Refining team practices
- Pausing AI expansion until foundations improve

The experiment is successful if it produces **learning that improves future decisions.**

---

# What This Experiment Is Really Testing

This work is not about introducing a new process.

It is testing a hypothesis:

> **AI multiplies the capabilities of the system it enters.**

If that hypothesis is correct, organizations must strengthen delivery foundations **before scaling AI adoption**.

---

- What signals you used
